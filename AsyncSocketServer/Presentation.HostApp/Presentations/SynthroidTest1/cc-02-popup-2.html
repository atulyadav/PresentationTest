Study Design: Adapted from a 1988 biochemical study conducted in
21 adult Caucasian patients in the United Kingdom with primary
hypothyroidism who were tested on a series of different thyroxine
dosages to evaluate effectiveness of measuring TSH levels to monitor
thyroid function. The "optimal dose" was determined for each patient
as that dosage of thyroxine being taken when the thyrotropin-releasing
hormone (TRH) response was normal (ie, an increase in TSH between 4.7
and 25 mIU/L). Biochemical assessment incorporated measurement of
serum TSH, T3, and T4. TSH lower limit of quantification was 0.2 mIU/L
and upper limit of normal was 5.6 mIU/L, as indicated by the shaded area.
Each dot represents 1 patient.
