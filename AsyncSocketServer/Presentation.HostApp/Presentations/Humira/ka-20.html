Combined analysis

The primary efficacy endpoint showed a higher proportion of patients who remained on KALETRA had HIV RNA < 50 copies/mL, indicating raltegravir did not establish noninferiority.
In the combined analysis, 84.4% of patients in the raltegravir arms (95% CI, 80.2–88.1) achieved viral load <50 copies/mL compared to 90.6% of patients in the KALETRA arms (95% CI, 87.1–93.5).
32 patients had confirmed virologic failures (HIV RNA >50 copies/mL) in the raltegravir group compared to 17 patients in the KALETRA group.

SWITCHMRK was terminated due to lower-than-expected virological efficacy for patients switched to raltegravir.
