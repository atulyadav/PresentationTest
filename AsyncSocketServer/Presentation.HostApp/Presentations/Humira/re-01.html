Study 730
KALETRA QD vs BID in na誰ve patients

An open-label, randomized, multicenter, phase 3 study
of KALETRA dosed once daily (QD) vs twice daily (BID)
+ TDF 300 mg/FTC 200 mg in treatment-na誰ve patients.2

Primary endpoint

Proportion of patients with HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL at week 48, (ITT; NC=F) approach comparing QD and BID groups with prespecified non-inferiority margin: 12%.

Results and Virologic Failures

At week 48, 77% of QD-dosed patients vs 76% of BID-
dosed patients had HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL (P=0.715).

10% of patients in the QD group and 8% of patients
in the BID group experienced virologic failure through
week 48 (includes confirmed viral rebound and failure
to achieve <50 copies/mL).

Once-daily dosing considerations

Do not administer once-daily KALETRA in pediatric patients; in adult patients with three or more lopinavir resistance substitutions; or in combination with efavirenz, nevirapine, nelfinavir, carbamazepine, phenobarbital, or phenytoin.

Patients should be informed that there may be greater chance of developing diarrhea with the once-daily
regimen as compared with the twice-daily regimen.

Study 720
7-year Open-Label Extension (OLE)
KALETRA study in ARV-na誰ve patients

A phase 2, dose-ranging, randomized, multicenter
trial evaluating the safety and efficacy of KALETRA
soft-gel capsules with stavudine and lamivudine at
3 dose levels within 2 groups of treatment-na誰ve
patients with confirmed HIV-1 infection. The study
consisted of a double-blind study period and an
open-label study period (n=100).3

Primary endpoint

Proportion of patients with plasma HIV-1 RNA
<400 copies/mL or <50 copies/mL at week 24 and
the duration of virologic response through week 48.

The study was extended with an open-label follow-up period through 360 weeks.

Results and Adverse Events

At 7 years, by ITT analysis, 61% had plasma HIV-1 RNA <400 copies/mL and 59% had <50 copies/mL.

In KALETRA clinical trials, the most common adverse reactions reported in >5% of adult patients were diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain, asthenia, vomiting, headache, and dyspepsia. In Study 720, lipodystrophy was also reported.
