Study 730
KALETRA QD vs BID in ARV-naïve patients

An open-label, randomized, multicenter, phase 3 study of KALETRA dosed once daily (QD) vs twice daily (BID) + tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF) 300 mg emtricitabine/(FTC) 200 mg
in ARV-naïve patients.2

Primary endpoint

Proportion of patients with HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL at week 48, intent-to-treat, non-completer=failure (ITT, NC=F) with a pre-specified non-inferiority margin of 12%.

Safety Considerations

In Study 730, the most common moderate to severe adverse events (occurring in ≥2% of patients) were diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, and headache. Other common adverse events reported in KALETRA clinical trials include abdominal pain, asthenia, and dyspepsia.

Once-daily dosing considerations

Do not administer once-daily KALETRA in pediatric patients; in adult patients with three or more lopinavir
resistance-associated substitutions; or in combination with efavirenz, nevirapine, nelfinavir,
carbamazepine, phenobarbital or phenytoin.

Patients should be informed that there may be a greater chance of developing diarrhea with the once-daily
regimen as compared with the twice-daily regimen.
