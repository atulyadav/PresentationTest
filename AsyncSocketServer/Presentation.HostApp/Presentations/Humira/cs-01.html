CASTLE
KALETRA vs ATV/RTV in ARV-naïve patients

International, multicenter, open-label, randomized, 96-week study to determine
the comparative clinical efficacy and safety of atazanavir/ritonavir (ATV/RTV) and
KALETRA in treatment-naïve HIV-1–infected patients.2,3

Primary endpoint

Proportion of patients with HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL at week 48 (ITT-CVR,* NC=F*).

Primary objective

Demonstrate non-inferiority of ATV/RTV once daily vs KALETRA twice daily based on primary endpoint if the lower limit of the confidence interval was greater than –10%.

*ITT-CVR, intent-to-treat, confirmed virologic response; NC=F, non-completer equals failure.
