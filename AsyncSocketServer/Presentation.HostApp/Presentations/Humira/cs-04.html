CASTLE: Lipid changes in treatment-naïve patients
Mean fasting lipid levels at baseline and week 963,4

Mean % changes in fasting TC,
non-HDL, and TG from baseline
at week 96 were significantly higher
in the KALETRA arm than the atazanavir/ritonavir arm (P<0.0001).3

25% of patients receiving KALETRA
had total cholesterol ≥240 mg/dL
and 4% had triglycerides >750 mg/dL at week 96, compared with 11% and <1% for the atazanavir/ritonavir group.3

2% of atazanavir/ritonavir vs 9% of KALETRA patients initiated lipid-lowering therapy after the start of study therapy.3

Safety Considerations

Treatment with KALETRA has resulted
in large increases in concentrations
of total cholesterol and triglycerides.
Monitor lipids prior to therapy and
periodically thereafter.

KALETRA is contraindicated with
lovastatin or simvastatin.

Treat patients for lipid elevations
as clinically appropriate.
