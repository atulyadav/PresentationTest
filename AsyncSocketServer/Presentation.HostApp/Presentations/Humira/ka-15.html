Mean increases for total cholesterol from baseline through week 48 were 29 mg/dL and 34.5 mg/dL in QD and BID groups, respectively (P=0.044); the mean increases for triglycerides were 44.8 mg/dL and 63.6 mg/dL in QD and BID groups, respectively (P=NS).4
In both treatment arms combined, 7 patients (1.1%) had total cholesterol >300 mg/dL and 9 patients (1.4%) had triglycerides >750 mg/dL.4

Safety Considerations

Treatment with KALETRA has resulted in large increases in concentrations of total cholesterol and triglycerides. Monitor lipids prior to therapy and periodically thereafter.
KALETRA is contraindicated with lovastatin and simvastatin.
Treat patients for lipid elevations as clinically appropriate.
