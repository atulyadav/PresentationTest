ARTEMIS2,3
KALETRA vs DRV/RTV in ARV-naïve patients

Randomized, open-label, phase III trial evaluating the efficacy and safety
of darunavir/ritonavir (DRV/RTV) 800/100 mg once daily versus KALETRA
in treatment-naïve, HIV-1–infected patients.

Non-inferiority of DRV/RTV was established if the lower limit of the confidence interval was greater
than –12% at 48 and 96 weeks.

Primary endpoint

Proportion of patients with HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL at week 48 (per protocol, time to loss of
virological response [TLOVR]).

Primary Objective

Demonstrate non-inferiority of DRV/RTV once daily vs KALETRA (BID or QD)
based on that primary endpoint.

Secondary Objective

Evaluation of other virologic and immunologic parameters (including proportion
of patients with HIV-1 RNA less than 400 copies/mL, change in HIV-1 RNA and CD4 cell count
change from baseline).

In the event of non-inferiority, testing for superiority of DRV/r over LPV/r (planned analysis).
